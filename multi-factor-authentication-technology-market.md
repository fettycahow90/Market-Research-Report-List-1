<p><p>Multi-factor authentication (MFA) enhances security by requiring multiple forms of verification. The market is segmented by the number of authentication factors used. Two-factor authentication (2FA) combines something you know (e.g., a password) with something you have (e.g., a mobile device). Three-factor authentication (3FA) adds a third element, like biometric data. Four-factor authentication (4FA) incorporates an additional layer, often involving location data. Five-factor authentication (5FA) further enhances security with multiple biometric identifiers or behavioral analytics, addressing advanced threats.</p></p>
